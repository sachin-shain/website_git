In January 2025, President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva appointed Brazilian diplomat André Corrêa do Lago [pt] as president of COP30.[11] The decision was welcomed by climate activists, given Lago’s experience in climate negotiations and leadership in climate justice discussions.[12] Lago is a veteran diplomat at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and has served as a longtime climate negotiator.On 4 November 2025, President Lula da Silva signed a law temporarily symbolically transferring the Brazilian national capital from Brasília to Belém for the duration of COP30, from 11 to 21 November 2025. During this period, all acts and orders from the President, ministers, and other federal agencies must be signed and registered in Belém. Additionally, the executive, legislative, and judiciary branches may conduct their activities from the COP30 host city.[14] The law was approved by the Brazilian National Congress on 7 October 2025
